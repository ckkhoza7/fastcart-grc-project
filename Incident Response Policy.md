**Incident Response Policy**

**Purpose:** The Purpose of this Incident Response Policy is to outline the correct response to how FastCart.Inc identifies, responds to, and recovers from Cybersecurity incidents to minimize impact and to restore normal operations quickly

**Scope:** This policy applies to all employees, contractors, and systems within FastCart.Inc. This includes cloud infrastructure, endpoints, web applications, and administrative accounts

**NIST Alignment:** This policy aligns with the Detect, Respond, and Recover functions of the NIST Cybersecurity Framework which identifies security incidents, contains the threats, and restores the affect system

**Incident Response Process**

1. Detection and Identification (Detect)  
   1. Security alerts from monitoring tools (e.g. Splunk) must be reviewed promptly  
   2. Potential incidents must be alerted to IT/security team immediately  
   3. Indicators of Compromise (IoC) must be documents  
2. Containment (Respond)  
   1. Compromised systems must be isolated from the environment upon detection  
   2. Compromised accounts must be disabled immediately  
   3. Temporary controls may be applied to prevent further damage  
3. Eradication (Respond)  
   1. Malware and unauthorized access must be removed from affected systems  
   2. Root of the problem must be identified and addressed  
4. Recovery (Recover)  
   1. Systems must be restored from clean backups  
   2. Services must be approved before moving forth with production  
   3. Enhanced monitoring must be applied during recovery  
5. Post Incident Review (Improve)  
   1. A root cause analysis of the incident must be performed  
   2. Lessons learned need to be documented  
   3. Security controls must be updated to prevent recurrence

**Roles and Responsibilities**

1. Security Team \- Incident Detection and Response  
2. IT Team \- System containment and recovery  
3. Employees \- Report suspicious activity immediately  
4. Management \- Approve critical response actions

**Incident Security Levels**

- Low: Minor disruption or issue  
- Medium: Limited security impact  
- High: Active compromise or data exposure  
- Critical: Major breach or or system-wide outage

**Review Cycle:** This policy will be reviewed annually or after any major security incident
